# The Core Four Lead Sources

Alex Hormozi's *$100M Leads* argues that every marketing activity that has ever generated a lead collapses into **four archetypes**. If you understand the four, you don't need to memorize tactics — you need to pick your archetype and go deep.

> "There are only four things you can do to get leads. I call them the Core Four."
> *— Alex Hormozi, $100M Leads*

## The two axes

The Core Four is a 2×2:

|              | **Warm** (people who know you)         | **Cold** (people who don't)                  |
|--------------|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **One-to-one**  | Warm outreach                            | Cold outreach                                  |
| **One-to-many** | Post free content                        | Run paid ads                                   |

That's it. Those four boxes contain everything. Let's walk each one.

## 1. Warm Outreach (one-to-one, warm)

You personally reach out to people who already know who you are: past customers, friends, old colleagues, your network. The highest-converting, lowest-scaling channel on the planet. Most founders ignore it because it feels awkward to ask people they know.

**Why it works:** the conversation starts with trust already in place. You're not interrupting; you're reconnecting.

**When to use it:** always, but especially in the first 90 days of a new offer, when you need signal and social proof more than scale.

**Tactical:** pull your entire contact list. Message one person per day. Don't pitch — ask if they (or someone they know) might have the problem you solve. Track every conversation.

## 2. Cold Outreach (one-to-one, cold)

You reach out to people who don't know you. Cold email, cold DMs, cold LinkedIn, cold calls. High effort per contact, moderate conversion, scales linearly with the number of outreach hours you invest.

**Why it works:** if you have a sharp target profile and a specific value hypothesis, you can skip past the noise of broad marketing and put your offer in front of exactly the people who should care.

**When to use it:** when you know exactly who your buyer is and have a clear, specific value hypothesis. Not when you're still hunting for product-market fit.

**Tactical:** see the `cold-email` skill on this site for execution patterns. Hormozi's extra insight: cold outreach doubles in effectiveness when paired with a free, fast, specific offer ("I'll audit your X in 15 minutes, no pitch") rather than a generic sales message.

## 3. Post Free Content (one-to-many, warm)

You publish useful content to an audience that has chosen to follow you: newsletter, YouTube, LinkedIn, podcast, blog, TikTok. Leads trickle out over time as followers decide to buy. The original long game.

**Why it works:** you build trust and authority at scale. One great piece of content can keep generating leads for years. Compounds.

**When to use it:** when you're willing to commit to 12+ months of consistent publishing before expecting meaningful lead flow. Also when your founder voice is a genuine differentiator.

**Tactical:** pick one format and one platform, publish relentlessly on a schedule you can sustain for 2 years. See `content-strategy`, `social-content`. Hormozi's core content rule: **give away your best stuff for free**. The people who will hire you or buy from you are not the people who would have figured it out themselves — they're the ones who see how much you give away and conclude "if the free stuff is this good, imagine the paid stuff."

## 4. Run Paid Ads (one-to-many, cold)

You pay a platform to put your message in front of strangers. Meta, Google, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, Reddit. Scales with dollars, produces data fast, requires a working offer and a working funnel.

**Why it works:** the only channel that reliably turns money directly into leads on a short timeline.

**When to use it:** when you have (a) a Grand Slam Offer that converts warmer traffic, (b) a funnel that doesn't leak, and (c) a clear target audience. Run ads **after** you've patched the funnel, not before.

**Tactical:** see `paid-ads` and `ad-creative`. Hormozi's warning: paid ads amplify whatever's already there. A weak offer with paid traffic just loses money faster.

## How the Core Four maps to the 19 Traction Channels

If Weinberg's 19 channels feel overwhelming (see `traction-19-channels`), Hormozi's Core Four is a useful simplification:

- **Warm outreach** ≈ existing network, referrals (part of viral marketing)
- **Cold outreach** ≈ sales, cold email
- **Post free content** ≈ content marketing, SEO, email marketing, engineering as marketing, podcasts, speaking, community building
- **Run paid ads** ≈ SEM, social ads, display, offline ads

Many founders find it easier to start with the Core Four, pick **one quadrant that matches their strengths**, and only return to the 19-channel list when that quadrant starts to plateau.

## The Hormozi rule: do one well before you do two

The single most common Core Four mistake: doing all four badly at the same time. Hormozi's rule is explicit — **master one quadrant before adding another**. "Master" means you can predictably and repeatedly generate leads from that source at a cost you can afford. Only then do you add a second channel, because the second channel will demand the same level of attention the first one needed.

This maps cleanly onto Weinberg's inner ring (the Bullseye Framework's "what's working") and Weinberg's Critical Path (focus on one channel at a time).

## Checklist

- [ ] Identify which quadrant of the Core Four you've actually been working (be honest — most founders do 30% of all four)
- [ ] Pick **one** quadrant to master in the next 90 days
- [ ] Set a daily or weekly activity target for that quadrant (emails sent, posts published, ad spend, etc.)
- [ ] Track leads generated per dollar or per hour invested
- [ ] Only add a second quadrant once the first is producing a predictable, affordable lead flow
- [ ] When testing a new channel, make sure your offer and funnel are already strong (see `hormozi-grand-slam-offer`, `traction-leaky-bucket-funnel`)
